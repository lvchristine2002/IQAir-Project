Understanding Air Quality

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a measurement scale used to describe air pollution levels. Here's what the values mean:

  • AQI 0-50: Good air quality with minimal risk to health.
  • AQI 51-100: Moderate air quality; sensitive groups may experience minor effects.
  • AQI 101-150: Unhealthy for sensitive groups; people with respiratory conditions should limit outdoor activities.
  • AQI 151-200: Unhealthy air for everyone; limit outdoor exposure.
  • AQI 201-300: Very unhealthy; health warnings for all groups.
  • AQI 301+: Hazardous; emergency conditions, everyone should stay indoors.
