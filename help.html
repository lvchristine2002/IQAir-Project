Air quality index is a scale used to communicate the quality of air and its potential health effects based on the concentration of various air pollutants, including ground-level ozone, particulate matter, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide.

Air Quality Index Levels:

AQI 0-50: Good

Air Quality: Satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.

Health Effects: None expected for the general population


AQI 51-100: Moderate

Air Quality: Acceptable; however, there may be a concern for some pollutants for a very small number of individuals who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.

Health Effects: No significant impact on health for the majority of the population.


AQI 101-150: Unhealthy for sensitive groups

Air Quality: Air quality may be a concern for people with respiratory or heart conditions, children, and older adults.

Health Effects: Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects, but the general public is not likely to be affected.


AQI 151-200:Unhealthy

Air Quality: Everyone may begin to experience health effects, and members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.

Health Effects: More serious effects can occur, especially for those with pre-existing conditions.


AQI 201-300:Very Unhealthy

Air Quality: Health Alert; everyone may experience more serious health effects

Health Effects: Increased respiratory effects and possibly more substantial health warnings for the general public.


AQI 301-500:Hazardous

Air Quality: Serious health effects for the entire population.

Health Effects: Health warnings of emergency conditions. The entire population is more likely to be affected, and outdoor activities should be limited.
